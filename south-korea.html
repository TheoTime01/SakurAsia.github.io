South Korea

Temple quiet meets neon nights—discover Seoul, Busan, and Jeju with insiders who know.

Sample Itinerary Book Now

Why Visit South Korea

Bold flavors, deep traditions, and forward-thinking design—an ideal place to explore contrast and craft.

From the royal palaces of Seoul and the beaches of Busan to the volcanic landscapes of Jeju, South Korea blends living heritage with a dynamic creative scene. We curate calm temples, countryside hikes, contemporary galleries, and the city’s best food—from pojangmacha late-night snacks to elegant, modern hansik.

Trip Highlights

  • Bukchon & Palaces

    Walk hanok lanes, then step into Gyeongbokgung’s grand courtyards with a local historian.

  • Busan Markets

    From Jagalchi to BIFF Square—street snacks, sea views, and café culture.

  • Jeju Nature

    Lava tubes, tea fields, waterfalls—and gentle coastal hikes on the Olle trails.

  • K-Food Scene

    Hands-on cooking, temple cuisine tastings, and modern Korean fine dining.

Best Time to Visit

Korea shines in all seasons—choose your mood.

Spring (Mar–May)

Cherry blossoms, mild temps—ideal for city walking and countryside temples.

Summer (Jun–Aug)

Beach days in Busan & Jeju; plan for warmth and the occasional shower.

Autumn (Sep–Nov)

Peak foliage, clear skies—prime hiking and photography season.

Winter (Dec–Feb)

Snowy mountains, cozy cafés, illuminated festivals; bundle up and enjoy.

Sample 7-Day Itinerary

Balanced pace with culture, food, and nature.

  1. Day 1 — Arrive Seoul

    Airport pickup, hanok stay orientation, evening stroll in Insadong.

  2. Day 2 — Palaces & Tea

    Gyeongbokgung, Bukchon walk, traditional tea ceremony, gallery hopping.

  3. Day 3 — Flavors of Seoul

    Market tastings (Gwangjang), hands-on cooking class, sunset at Namsan.

  4. Day 4 — KTX to Busan

    Jagalchi Market, Gamcheon village, seaside temple at Haedong Yonggungsa.

  5. Day 5 — Coastal Leisure

    Haeundae beach walk, cafés, craft beer, spa time (jjimjilbang optional).

  6. Day 6 — Fly to Jeju

    Olle trail segment, tea field visit, local black pork dinner.

  7. Day 7 — Jeju & Depart

    Manjanggul lava tube, waterfall stop, flight back via Seoul.

All trips are private or small-group and fully customizable to your pace and interests.

Practical Info

Getting Around

High-speed KTX links major cities; T-money cards make metro/bus seamless. Private transfers available.

Language

Korean is widely spoken; English signage common in cities. Guides bridge any gaps.

Connectivity

eSIM/pocket Wi-Fi ensure smooth navigation and translation on the go.

Etiquette

Polite bowing, two-handed exchanges, and shoe removal in some spaces—your guide will advise.
